The Stewardship Mapping and Assessment (STEW-MAP) project visualizes groups that help take care of the environment on a digital map. Our Bridger-Teton STEW-MAP project closed in 2021, resulting in a mapped database. This webpage is focused on keeping the contact information of the mapped groups up to date and to identify new groups to map. To edit contact information of both already mapped groups (circled in green) and registered non-profits with the IRS, find existing groups on the right-hand list. You can also add a new group by clicking the button saying "Click here to add a group to the map".

Your participation in STEW-MAP is completely voluntary.

This form is to collect basic information on groups' basic contact information, including where each group office or meeting space is located. You can enter your own group's information or other groups that you know of or collaborate with. Your participation in STEW-MAP is completely voluntary.
